Allowance Rate Breakdowns

By Art Unit (Top 10)
share of allowances by # actions (0/1/2/3/4+)
Methodology. Recency window applies to the cohort-axis date selected above; rows whose cohort date falls outside the window are excluded entirely from the analytics on this tab. The headline metrics describe the distribution of allowances by office-action count: of all allowed applications (status Patented, NOA Mailed, or Allowed for Issue) with a verifiable file history, what share were allowed with 0 / 1 / 2 / 3 / 4+ rejecting office actions in their file history. An "action" here is a non-final OA or a final rejection — RCEs are procedural and aren't counted as actions. The five buckets are mutually exclusive and exhaustive, so shares sum to 100% (modulo allowed apps with no application_analytics row, which are reported separately). Pendency uses still-pending applications. Foreign-priority share is true when the Biblio XML has a ForeignPriorityList entry or any PCT national-stage parent.

By Extension Length

How many additional 1-month USPTO extension blocks each late response consumed. A response filed any amount past the statutory deadline counts as ≥1 month; a response 1 day past 1 month + the deadline counts as 2 months; etc.

Year 1 Month 2 Months 3 Months 4+ Months Total
Methodology. An "extension" here is a heuristic proxy: a qualifying applicant response (response, RCE, or Notice of Appeal) filed strictly more than 3 calendar months after the CTNF or CTFR mail date, or more than 2 calendar months after a Restriction Requirement (CTRS). Each extension is bucketed by the calendar year of the response. Per office action we look at the first qualifying response between the OA mail date and the earlier of the next examiner action or the first Notice of Allowance. For CTRS, an applicant Election (IFW ELC. / ELECTION) or Remarks/Argument document (IFW REM) mailed after the CTRS also closes the response window — and counts as a CTRS response itself when no other classified response is found — so the actual Election against the restriction is captured even though prosecution_events doesn't classify it, and a far-later merits response to a subsequent CTNF/CTFR doesn't get credited back to the restriction. Counts the full chip-filtered selection — not affected by the recency window on the Allowance Analytics tab. The current calendar year is YTD; the lighter dashed extension above the bar is a linear projection (actual ÷ fraction of year elapsed) of the full-year total, distributed across CTNF/CTFR/Restriction segments in the same proportions as the YTD actuals. Filings by Type
PROVISIONAL · REGULAR · CON · DIV · CIP · DESIGN · OTHER
Methodology. Each application is bucketed by its USPTO application-number series (provisional 60–63/, design 29/, reissue/reexam 35/90/95/96/ bucketed as other) and, for non-prefix series, by the row's own ContinuityDescription in the bibliographic XML (Continuation, Continuation-in-Part, or Divisional). Rows whose type is still being backfilled are bucketed as regular so per-year totals stay byte-identical to the chart above. The current calendar year is YTD; the lighter dashed extension above each segment is a linear projection of the full-year total, distributed across types in the same proportions as the YTD actuals. Top Applicants
RANKED BY MOST RECENT YEAR · YOY GROWTH SHOWN ON THE RIGHT
Methodology. Filings bucket on the application's filing_date; rows with no filing date are excluded. The portfolio totals include every applicant in the current chip-filtered selection. The top-applicants matrix ranks by most-recent-year filings (with all-time total as a tiebreak) and caps at 20 rows. Growth (Δ %) for past years compares full-year totals; for the current calendar year it compares year-to-date filings to the same date-of-year span in the prior year, so a high-volume applicant isn't flagged as "down 95%" on January 5th. A dash (—) means there is no prior baseline (first year in the window or the prior year had zero filings for that applicant).
